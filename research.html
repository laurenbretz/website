Ongoing Research

Michigan Blight Elimination Study

Michigan State University Land Policy Institute

Description: This study will evaluate the economic and social effects of federal Hardest Hit blight elimination funds in sixteen Michigan cities.

My contribution:

  • create a literature review on other blight elimination efforts and results
  • analyze city data on vacancy, foreclosure, foreclosure assistance, and crime
  • conduct and analyze surveys of residents' perceptions in each city

Regional Services Recommendation Study

Michigan State University Land Policy Institute

Description: LPI is developing a catalogue of state, regional, and local services in Michigan's Region 2 as a part of Networks Northwest's Regional Prosperity Initiative. This includes an analysis of coverage, efficiency, and inter-agency dynamics.

My contribution:

  • conduct interviews with representatives of studied organizations
  • synthesize interview results to create recommendations for state, regional, and local entities

View News Writeup

Michigan Renaissance Zones Assessment

Michigan State University Land Policy Institute

Description: LPI will analyze the effectiveness of the Michigan Economic Development Corporation's Renaissance Zone Program with respect to number of new jobs created, change in taxable value and state equalized value, average wage of new jobs, and change in income of area residents.

My contribution:

  • create a literature review on other tax incentives programs and their effectiveness
  • collect and graphically represent Michigan's tax incentive spending by category

Placemaking Score and Tool

Michigan State University Land Policy Institute

Description: LPI will catalogue and analyze the effectiveness and accessibility of the most common placemaking assessments and indices. The goal of this study is to deliver a recommendation for a meaningful, comparable, and maintainable placemaking score for the State of Michigan.

My contribution:

  • create a lookup of existing scoring systems and indices for evaluating communities' "sense of place"
  • determine pros and cons for each score with regard to time investment, monetary cost, data availability, and predictive ability
